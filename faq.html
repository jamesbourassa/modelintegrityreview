What to expect

Model Integrity Review (MIR) is a fixed-scope, management-led, document-based review of an analytical model and its supporting documentation. MIR focuses on structural clarity, internal consistency, assumption traceability, and documentation sufficiency for management oversight, audit, or decision use.
MIR complements existing validation or governance programs by focusing on clarity and defensibility of the model as documented, not statistical testing or regulatory compliance.

Scope boundaries (read first)

  • MIR does not build, modify, execute, or optimize models.
  • MIR does not validate model correctness or performance.
  • MIR does not certify, approve, or endorse models or outcomes.
  • MIR does not provide regulatory sign-off or legal opinions.
  • MIR does not support litigation, testimony, or media strategy.
If validation, certification, or redesign is required, MIR will say so directly.

Engagement steps

1) Fit and materials check
You send a short note describing the model’s decision context, who needs to stand behind it, and what documentation exists today. MIR confirms whether a document-based review is a good fit and identifies the materials needed.
2) Scope framing and fixed fee
MIR proposes a fixed scope (what materials will be reviewed, what will not be reviewed, and what questions the memorandum will address) and confirms a fixed fee before work begins. Engagements are designed to be contained decision-support controls.
3) Review (document-first)
MIR examines whether the model is coherent and defensible as documented:
  • Structural clarity: whether the structure can be understood from the materials without undocumented “tribal knowledge.”
  • Internal consistency: whether definitions, logic, and dependencies hold together across sections and artifacts.
  • Assumption traceability: whether key assumptions are visible and traceable to outputs and decisions.
  • Documentation sufficiency: whether an oversight reader could follow the reasoning and limitations.
4) Deliverable: short advisory memorandum
MIR delivers a short written review memorandum describing review scope, observed strengths, and documentation or structural risks. The memorandum is advisory and is not an endorsement, certification, or validation. Depth is driven by model complexity and documentation quality—not a fixed reporting template.
5) Optional clarification (limited)
If helpful, MIR can answer a small number of clarification questions about the memorandum’s statements. MIR does not iterate into redesign, optimization, or validation work.

Confidentiality

Client materials are treated as confidential. MIR does not disclose client identities or reuse client materials across engagements. Any internal drafting or analytical tools are used only as aids; all judgment, interpretation, and conclusions are made by the reviewer.
