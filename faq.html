What exactly does Model Integrity Review evaluate?
MIR evaluates the structural clarity, internal consistency, traceability, and documentation quality of analytical models and supporting materials. The focus is on whether a model is understandable and defensible as a decision artifact.
Do you validate or certify model correctness?
No. MIR does not validate technical correctness, certify compliance, or endorse conclusions. The review is structural and documentation-focused only.
Is this a technical or domain-specific review?
No. MIR does not redesign models, debug code, or optimize performance. The review examines how the model is documented, structured, and explained — not whether it is “right.”
What types of models are appropriate?
Analytical, financial, engineering, simulation-based, policy, and research models may all be appropriate, provided the review remains at the documentation and structural level.
How is the review conducted?
All reviews are document-based and conducted remotely. MIR reviews only the materials provided and does not execute or run models.
What is the deliverable?
A concise written memorandum outlining scope, materials reviewed, observed strengths, documentation and traceability risks, and non-prescriptive recommendations, with clear limitations and disclaimers.
How do we get started?
Send a brief email describing the model’s purpose, intended audience, and available materials. MIR will confirm fit and scope by return email.
