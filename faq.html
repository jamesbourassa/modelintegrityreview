Frequently Asked Questions

Home Process FAQ
What is Model Integrity Review?
MIR provides independent, management-led, document-based review of analytical models, focused on structural clarity, internal consistency, assumption traceability, and documentation sufficiency for management oversight.
What do we get at the end?
A short written review memorandum describing scope, observed strengths, and documentation or structural risks. Advisory only — not an endorsement, certification, or validation.
Is this validation or audit?
No. MIR does not validate correctness, certify compliance, or approve models. It assesses whether a model is coherent and defensible as documented.
Do you need our code or data?
Usually no. Reviews rely on existing documentation and materials. MIR does not run models or generate new data.
Who performs the review?
All reviews are led, written, and finalized by a single Lead Reviewer to preserve independence and accountability.
When is MIR not appropriate?
When validation, regulatory sign-off, litigation support, or technical redesign is required.
