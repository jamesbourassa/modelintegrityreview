Model Integrity Review engagements are independent, document-based, and advisory. They do not involve model execution, validation, certification, or redesign.

  1. Initial Inquiry & Scope Screening
    A brief exchange confirms the nature of the model, the intended use of the review, and whether the engagement fits MIR’s scope and boundaries.
  2. Engagement Confirmation
    Scope, timeline, deliverables, and fee are confirmed in writing before work begins. Reviews proceed only after mutual agreement on boundaries and expectations.
  3. Materials Intake
    Client-provided documents are received and logged. These materials form the sole basis of the review; no external inference or model execution is performed.
  4. Structural Model Mapping
    The documented structure of the model is mapped at a conceptual level, including inputs, assumptions, transformations, and outputs as described in the materials.
  5. Consistency, Traceability & Clarity Review
    Documentation is examined for internal consistency, assumption visibility, terminology alignment, and traceability between narrative descriptions and structure.
  6. Review Memorandum Preparation
    A concise written memorandum (typically 2–4 pages) summarizes observations, highlights documentation or governance risks, and notes areas where clarity may be improved. Findings are descriptive and non-prescriptive.
  7. Delivery
    The memorandum is delivered electronically to the client. Use and distribution are at the client’s discretion, subject to engagement terms.

Model Integrity Review is designed to support understanding, explanation, and defensibility of models as documented — not to determine correctness or approve decisions.
