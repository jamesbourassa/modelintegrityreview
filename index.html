What We Mean by Model Integrity Review

Model Integrity Review (MIR) provides independent, management-led, document-based review of analytical models and their supporting documentation, focused on structural clarity, internal consistency, assumption traceability, and documentation sufficiency for management oversight, audit, or decision use.

MIR exists to answer a narrow but critical question:
Can leadership clearly understand, explain, and stand behind this model as it is currently documented?

MIR complements existing validation or governance programs by focusing on clarity and defensibility of the model as documented, not statistical testing or regulatory compliance.

MIR does not build, modify, run, or validate models. We review existing materials and provide a short written review memorandum describing scope, observed strengths, and documentation or structural risks. The memorandum is advisory and is not an endorsement, certification, or validation.

What we do
  • Independent analytical model reviews for management and oversight audiences
  • Structural integrity and internal consistency review
  • Assumption clarity and traceability checks
  • Unit / scaling coherence review (where applicable)
  • Documentation adequacy for management oversight, audit, or executive use
This is a structural and documentation review of the materials provided — not technical validation, optimization, or redesign.
What we do not do
  • Build, modify, execute, or optimize models
  • Validate model correctness or performance
  • Certify, approve, or endorse models
  • Provide regulatory sign-off or legal opinions
  • Support litigation, testimony, or media strategy
If validation, certification, or redesign is required, we will say so directly.
What you receive
  • A short written review memorandum describing review scope, observed strengths, and documentation or structural risks
  • Advisory conclusions — not an endorsement, certification, or validation
  • Depth driven by model complexity and documentation quality, not a fixed reporting template
Independence and accountability
  • MIR does not build or modify client models
  • MIR does not accept outcome-contingent fees
  • Disagreements and unresolved issues are documented explicitly
  • All reviews are led, written, and finalized by a single Lead Reviewer
When MIR is a good fit
  • A model is already in use and leadership needs defensibility
  • Documentation has grown organically and lacks coherence
  • A model is being handed off to executives, auditors, or regulators
  • Internal teams need an independent structural perspective before validation or audit
If you are seeking approval, certification, or technical optimization, MIR is unlikely to be the right fit.
